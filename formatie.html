The greatest player of all time Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro

Cristiano Ronaldo is a Portuguese professional footballer widely regarded as one of the greatest players of all time. Born on February 5, 1985, he is known for his incredible athleticism, goal-scoring ability, and dedication to fitness. Ronaldo has played for top clubs including Sporting CP, Manchester United, Real Madrid, Juventus, and currently Al-Nassr in Saudi Arabia. Over his career, he has won numerous league titles, five UEFA Champions League trophies, and multiple Ballon d'Or awards. He is also the all-time leading goal scorer for both Real Madrid and the Portugal national team.

Karim Mostafa Benzema

Karim Benzema is a French professional footballer widely regarded as one of the most intelligent and complete strikers of his generation. Born on December 19, 1987, he began his career at Lyon before moving to Real Madrid in 2009. During his time in Spain, he became the club’s second-highest all-time goal scorer and won numerous titles, including five UEFA Champions League trophies and multiple La Liga titles. Known for his vision, link-up play, and finishing, Benzema won the Ballon d'Or in 2022. He currently plays for Al-Ittihad in the Saudi Pro League.

Francisco Román Alarcón Suárez (Isco)

Isco is a Spanish professional footballer known for his creativity, dribbling, and technical skill as an attacking midfielder. Born on April 21, 1992, he began his career at Valencia and gained recognition at Málaga before joining Real Madrid in 2013. At Madrid, he won numerous trophies, including five UEFA Champions League titles and multiple La Liga championships, becoming known for his flair and vision on the ball. After leaving in 2022, he had a short spell at Sevilla and later revived his career at Real Betis, returning to top form in La Liga.

Toni Kroos

Toni Kroos is a German professional footballer renowned for his passing accuracy, vision, and composure in midfield. Born on January 4, 1990, he began his senior career at Bayern Munich, where he won multiple Bundesliga titles and the UEFA Champions League. In 2014, he joined Real Madrid, becoming a key part of their midfield alongside Luka Modrić and Casemiro. At Madrid, he won five Champions League titles and several domestic trophies. Kroos also played a vital role in Germany’s 2014 FIFA World Cup victory. He retired from club football in 2024 after Euro 2024.

Carlos Henrique Casimiro

Casemiro is a Brazilian professional footballer known for his strength, defensive intelligence, and leadership as a defensive midfielder. Born on February 23, 1992, he began his European career with Real Madrid, becoming a crucial part of the club’s success alongside Toni Kroos and Luka Modrić. At Madrid, he won five UEFA Champions League titles and multiple La Liga trophies, earning a reputation as one of the best holding midfielders in the world. In 2022, he joined Manchester United, bringing experience and stability to their midfield and winning the EFL Cup in his first season.

Luka Modrić

Luka Modrić is a Croatian professional footballer known for his vision, passing, and control as a central midfielder. Born on September 9, 1985, he began his career at Dinamo Zagreb before moving to Tottenham Hotspur and later joining Real Madrid in 2012. At Madrid, he became a key figure in one of the most successful eras in the club’s history, winning multiple La Liga titles and five UEFA Champions League trophies. Modrić won the Ballon d'Or in 2018 after leading Croatia to the World Cup final, becoming the first player in over a decade to break the Messi-Ronaldo streak.

Marcelo Vieira da Silva Júnior

Marcelo is a Brazilian professional footballer known for his attacking flair, dribbling, and creativity as a left-back. Born on May 12, 1988, he joined Real Madrid in 2007 and became one of the most decorated players in the club’s history. Marcelo won five UEFA Champions League titles, multiple La Liga trophies, and was widely regarded as one of the best left-backs of his generation. Renowned for his technical skill and leadership, he also had a successful international career with Brazil, representing his country in multiple World Cups.

Raphaël Xavier Varane

Raphaël Varane is a French professional footballer known for his pace, composure, and strength as a central defender. Born on April 25, 1993, he joined Real Madrid in 2011 as a teenager and quickly became a key part of their defense. During his time at the club, he won multiple La Liga titles and four UEFA Champions League trophies. Varane also played a crucial role in France's 2018 FIFA World Cup victory. In 2021, he moved to Manchester United, bringing experience and leadership to their back line. He retired from international football in 2023.

Sergio Ramos García

Sergio Ramos is a Spanish professional footballer known for his toughness, leadership, and goal-scoring ability as a central defender. Born on March 30, 1986, he joined Real Madrid in 2005 from Sevilla and became one of the club’s most iconic captains. Ramos won numerous trophies with Madrid, including five La Liga titles and four UEFA Champions League titles. He was also a key figure in Spain’s golden era, winning Euro 2008, World Cup 2010, and Euro 2012. Renowned for his clutch performances and aggressive style, he later played for PSG and returned to Sevilla in 2023.

Daniel Carvajal Ramos

Dani Carvajal is a Spanish professional footballer known for his relentless energy, defensive reliability, and attacking support as a right-back. Born on January 11, 1992, he came through Real Madrid’s youth academy, La Fábrica, and briefly played for Bayer Leverkusen before returning to Madrid in 2013. Since then, Carvajal has been a crucial part of the team’s defense, winning multiple La Liga titles and five UEFA Champions League trophies. Known for his intensity, tactical awareness, and consistency, he has also been a regular for the Spanish national team, representing his country in European Championships and World Cups.

Keilor Antonio Navas Gamboa

Keylor Navas is a Costa Rican professional footballer known for his quick reflexes, agility, and clutch performances as a goalkeeper. Born on December 15, 1986, he gained international attention with standout displays at the 2014 FIFA World Cup. Shortly after, he joined Real Madrid, where he became the starting goalkeeper and helped the team win three consecutive UEFA Champions League titles, along with several domestic trophies. Navas later played for Paris Saint-Germain and Nottingham Forest, continuing to perform at a high level. He is widely regarded as one of the best goalkeepers in CONCACAF history.
